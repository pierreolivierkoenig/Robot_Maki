Dynamixel Record and Playback Example

This example demonstrates how to capture sets of motor positions (frames) and then play them back. The playback feature also shows how to generate a synchronous write command that sets the goal position and velocity of all of the motors simultaneously.

Click here for an explanation of all of the front panel controls.

Frames and sequences

A Frame is a set of positions along with a step time. The positions are usually obtained from clicking Record. You can think of the Frame as a picture or pose that captures the motors' positions. A sequence then is like a movie, or a collection of frames. By stepping through the sequence, smooth motion is generated.

You can select the Frame by either entering it manually, using the increment/decrement control, or by clicking on a frame in the table.

Recording, deleting, inserting, copying, pasting, and modifying

Recording

When you click Record, the program will poll of the motors listed for their positions and append the position data as a frame to the end of the sequence. The Step Time is also integrated in that frame.

Inserting

When you click Insert, the program will run the same code as if you click Record, but instead will insert the data in the sequence at the specified Frame. The newly recorded frame will shift all of the other frames down.

Deleting

When you click Delete, the program will remove the specified Frame from the sequence.

Copying

Copying works a little differently than the other functions. You must select and highlight cells you want to copy--simply clicking a cell or frame is not enough. The program will copy the complete frame of whatever you highlight.

Pasting

Pasting works like Insert, but instead of inserting data just polled from the motors, pasting inserts the copied frames.

Modifying

If you want to change a step time or position, simply change the value on the table. You can even add completely new values.

Playback and initializing

When you click Play, the program will compile all of the position and time step data to generate sets of serial strings. The serial strings are synchronous write commands to the motors that command the goal position and velocity of every motor simultaneously. Each synchronous write command is sent out after the step time elapses. The motors' speeds are calculated so that they reach their goal position at the end of the time step.

When you click Play, the program will play back the sequence from Frame until the end. Since the program does not detect if the motors are currently positioned at Frame, it assumes they are. To make sure the motors are in the correct initial position, click Go before starting playback.

Click Stop Playback at any time to stop the progression of the motion through the frames in the sequence.

Motor torque control

You can click All On or All off to turn all of the motors' torques on or off. Alternatively "Page Up" and "Page Down" are keyboard shortcuts for the same function. Finally, you can turn the torque on or off for each motor under the motor torque control.

Saving and loading

Saving your work and progress is important. When you click Save, the program will store the positions, step times, motor list, and speed scale to file. Loading will bring back all of the same information.
